## Scope

``simsopt.field.magneticfieldclasses.Dommaschk`` and
``simsopt.field.magneticfieldclasses.Reiman`` to a new pure-JAX module
at ``src/simsopt/jax_core/analytic_fields.py``. The public wrapper

in scope here; that wrapper continues to call the C++ kernels and is
unchanged by this item.

## Kernels Ported

### Dommaschk vacuum field (W. Dommaschk, 1986, CPC 40, 203-218)

- Helper scalars ``alpha(m, l)``, ``alphas(m, l)``, ``beta(m, l)``,
  ``betas(m, l)``, ``gamma1(m, l)``, ``gammas(m, l)`` mirror lines
  4-58 of ``src/simsoptpp/dommaschk.cpp``.
- ``Dmn(m, n, R, Z)`` and ``Nmn(m, n, R, Z)`` (lines 60-84 of the C++
  source) are expanded at trace time into finite polynomial term
  lists ``{ (exp_R, exp_Z, exp_log, coeff) }``. Derivatives
  ``dRDmn``, ``dZDmn``, ``dRRDmn``, ``dZZDmn``, ``dRZDmn`` and the
  ``N``-side analogues (lines 86-245 of the C++ source) are obtained
  by symbolic Python differentiation of the same term lists; this
  matches the C++ closed-form expressions to floating-point
  precision.
- ``BR`` (line 264), ``BZ`` (line 281), ``Bphi`` (line 298) and the
  six first-order derivatives ``dRBR``, ``dZBZ``, ``dRBZ``, ``dZBR``,
  ``dRBphi``, ``dZBphi``, ``dphiBR``, ``dphiBZ``, ``dphiBphi`` are
  built from the precomputed term lists at trace time and consumed
  by the Cartesian assembly (lines 519-528 of the C++ source).
- The C++ identity ``dZBR(m, n, ...) == dRBZ(m, n, ...)`` (compare
  lines 400-432 of the source) is preserved.

### Reiman island model (Reiman & Greenside, 1986, CPC 43, 157-167)

- Cylindrical-frame ``BR``, ``BZ``, ``Bphi`` and their first
  derivatives mirror ``src/simsoptpp/reiman.cpp::ReimanB`` (lines
  8-42) and ``ReimandB`` (lines 44-106).
- ``k_theta`` is treated as static metadata so the per-mode loop is
  unrolled at trace time. ``iota0``, ``iota1``, ``epsilon`` and the
  point array are runtime data.

## Public surface

- :class:`DommaschkSpec` ``(m, n: tuple[int, ...]; coeffs:
  jax.Array of shape [K, 2])``.
- :func:`dommaschk_B(spec, points) -> [K, N, 3]` -- raw Cartesian
  field per mode.
- :func:`dommaschk_dB(spec, points) -> [K, N, 3, 3]` -- raw
  Cartesian gradient per mode with convention
  ``dB[k, p, i, j] = d B_j / d x_i``.
- :class:`ReimanSpec` ``(iota0, iota1, k_theta: tuple[int, ...],
  epsilon: jax.Array of shape [M], m0_symmetry: int)``.
- :func:`reiman_B(spec, points) -> [N, 3]`.
- :func:`reiman_dB(spec, points) -> [N, 3, 3]`.

Both Dommaschk outputs match ``sopp.DommaschkB`` / ``sopp.DommaschkdB``
exactly in shape and content (no ``ToroidalField(1, 1)`` baseline is
folded in). Both Reiman outputs match ``sopp.ReimanB`` /
``sopp.ReimandB`` in shape and content.

## JAX Strategy

- ``(m, n)`` for Dommaschk and ``(k_theta, m0_symmetry)`` for Reiman
  are JIT-static metadata. The kernel factories
  ``_dommaschk_B_kernel``, ``_dommaschk_dB_kernel``,
  ``_reiman_B_kernel``, ``_reiman_dB_kernel`` are cached via
  ``functools.lru_cache`` so each unique mode-index tuple recompiles
  exactly once.
- Per-mode kernels are decorated with ``jax.jit``. The Python loop
  over the ``K`` Dommaschk coefficient pairs is performed outside
  the JIT boundary (each call multiplexes a small number of
  compiled mode kernels).
- ``vmap`` and ``shard_map`` are not introduced; the point-axis
  ``N`` is handled with broadcasting over per-point dense arrays.
- Buffer donation is not used: all inputs are read-only.

## Math And Physics Invariants

- Cartesian Cartesian-frame ``B`` and ``dB`` (units: Tesla).
- ``R = sqrt(x^2 + y^2) > 0`` invariant (documented in the module
  docstring). Evaluating at ``R = 0`` is outside the kernel's
  domain (both ``Bphi/R`` and ``log(R)`` diverge there).
- ``dB`` is symmetric (vacuum-field property:
  ``dB[i, j] == dB[j, i]``); verified by
  ``test_dommaschk_grad_symmetric``.
- Reiman ``Bphi = -1`` is constant; ``dRBphi = dZBphi = dphiBphi
  = 0``.
