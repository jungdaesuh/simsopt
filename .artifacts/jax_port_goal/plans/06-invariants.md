# Item 06 Math And Physics Invariants

## Units And Scales

- Surface position arrays `gamma`, `gammadash1`, `gammadash2` are in
  metres. The closeout fixture uses `range="field period"` and a
  major-radius perturbation of `rc[0, ntor] = 1.25` plus axisymmetric
  and helical Fourier perturbations at amplitude scales 0.03 - 0.18 to
  produce a well-formed (non-self-intersecting) torus at production
  scale.
- `area_jax` returns an area in square metres; `volume_jax` returns a
  volume in cubic metres. Both come from the same trapezoidal /
  midpoint quadrature consumed by simsoptpp.
- Quadrature points `quadpoints_phi`, `quadpoints_theta` are unitless
  fractions of `[0, 1/nfp)` and `[0, 1)` respectively, generated by
  `Surface.get_phi_quadpoints(nphi=..., range="field period")` and
  `Surface.get_theta_quadpoints(ntheta=...)`. The closeout fixture
  uses `nphi=32, ntheta=16, range="field period"`.

## Orientation And Sign Conventions

- `normal = gammadash1 x gammadash2` is the unnormalized outward
  normal; its magnitude is the area element. Both `surface.normal()`
  (CPU) and `surface_rz_fourier_normal_from_*` (JAX) follow the same
  cross-product convention. The closeout test asserts
  componentwise parity of the normal vector array, so any sign flip
  would be caught at `direct_kernel` tolerance.
- `gammadash1` is the derivative with respect to the toroidal angle
  parameter (axis 0); `gammadash2` is the derivative with respect to
  the poloidal angle parameter (axis 1).

## Stellsym Coverage

- `stellsym=True` and `stellsym=False` SurfaceRZFourier kernels follow
  the CPU DOF ordering exactly: cos-cos + sin-sin for x (i.e. R cos phi
  - Z sin phi rewritten in tensor basis), cos-sin + sin-cos for y and
  z. The mapping is materialized by `stellsym_scatter_indices(mpol,
  ntor)` for the SurfaceXYZ(Tensor)Fourier path; the SurfaceRZFourier
  path uses an `rc`/`rs`/`zc`/`zs` block layout directly. Both
  conventions are validated by the existing
  `test_surface_rzfourier_jax_parity_stellsym`,
  `test_surface_rzfourier_jax_parity_non_stellsym`,
  `TestSurfaceFourierJaxCppParity::test_coefficient_derivatives_match_cpp`
  (parametrized over stellsym), and now by the production-scale
  closeout fixture for SurfaceRZFourier stellsym=False.
- Item 06 closes the gap: the pre-existing non-stellsym fixture
  `_make_surface(stellsym=False)` runs at `nphi=9, ntheta=10`, below
  the production-scale floor. The production-scale stellsym=True
  fixtures (`_make_hlo_probe_surface` at 17x18, Gauss-Bonnet at
  32x33) did not exercise the non-stellsym DOF block. The closeout
  fixture asserts both `rc` / `zs` (stellsym DOFs) and `rs` / `zc`
  (non-stellsym DOFs) at `nphi=32, ntheta=16, mpol=4, ntor=3, nfp=2`.

## Derivative Shape

- `gamma`, `gammadash1`, `gammadash2`, `normal`: shape
  `(nphi, ntheta, 3)`.
- `area`, `volume`, `mean_cross_sectional_area`, `major_radius`,
  `minor_radius`, `aspect_ratio`: scalar float64.
- `dgamma_by_dcoeff`: shape `(nphi, ntheta, 3, ndof)`. Not asserted by
  the closeout fixture; existing tests
  `test_surface_rzfourier_jax_jacobian_parity_*` cover it.

## Contract Type

`fixed_scalar` + `fixed_gradient_vjp`. The closeout fixture asserts a
fixed-state forward parity at the `direct_kernel` lane (rtol=1e-10,
atol=1e-12). Optimizer trajectory parity is not in scope for item 06.

## mpol / ntor Convention

`mpol` is the maximum poloidal mode number (`m = 0, 1, ..., mpol`).
`ntor` is the maximum toroidal mode number divided by `nfp`
(`n = -ntor, ..., -1, 0, 1, ..., ntor`, with `n=0` only as a
special-case constraint for `m=0`). The Fourier evaluator is
`r = sum r_{c,m,n} cos(m theta - nfp n phi) + r_{s,m,n} sin(...)`,
matching simsoptpp `SurfaceRZFourier` exactly. The closeout fixture
exercises `mpol=4, ntor=3` for both branches.

## Excluded Regimes

- Self-intersecting surfaces: the closeout fixture does not test
  self-intersection or zero-Jacobian degenerate cases. These are
  covered separately by
  `test_surface_rzfourier_unitnormal_degenerate_surface_matches_cpu_singularity`
  and by `test_surface.is_self_intersecting` (CPU oracle).
- Zero quadrature points or zero-DOF surfaces: not in scope; the
  `from_nphi_ntheta` constructor guarantees both are positive.

## Red Evidence (Coverage Witness)

The closeout fixture is a coverage-completeness witness, not a
bug-fix red step. The parent commit `a9da18fac` already produced
identical JAX/CPU outputs for stellsym=False SurfaceRZFourier at this
scale; the new test installs the assertion. This is the section-4c
carve-out for partial-coverage closeouts. The negative-control role
is filled by the lane-tolerance contract itself: any future regression
that introduces a sign flip, scale error, or stellsym-branch coupling
bug above `rtol=1e-10` will fail the new test before reaching
production.
